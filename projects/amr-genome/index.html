Antimicrobial Resistance Analysis抗生素抗藥性分析

Two ways to read resistance out of a bacterial genome — a research project in progress 兩種從細菌基因組讀出抗藥性的方法——一個持續進行中的研究專題

Active research · May 2024 – present持續研究中 · 2024 年 5 月至今
§ 01 / The problem問題

Resistance is written in the DNA抗藥性寫在 DNA 之中

Repo on GitHub →GitHub 源碼 →

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) is one of the more quietly serious problems in public health: bacteria evolve resistance to the antibiotics meant to kill them, and predicting which strains are resistant to which drugs — fast, and from genomic data alone — matters for both treatment decisions and surveillance. This project explores two different machine-learning approaches to the same underlying question: can a model read resistance directly out of a bacterial genome, using the public PATRIC genome database as its data source? 抗微生物抗藥性（AMR）是公共衛生領域一個看似安靜、實則嚴重的問題：細菌會對本應殺死它們的抗生素演化出抗藥性，而能否快速地、單憑基因組數據就預測哪些菌株對哪些藥物具抗藥性，對治療決策與監測都至關重要。這個專題探索兩種不同的機器學習方法，回答同一個根本問題：模型能否直接從細菌基因組讀出抗藥性，使用公開的 PATRIC 基因組資料庫作為數據來源？

Approach 1 · k-mer + classical ML方法一 · k-mer 特徵 + 傳統機器學習

A Kedro-orchestrated PySpark pipeline breaks each genome down into overlapping k-mers (short DNA subsequences, k=11) and counts their frequencies as features — a standard, well-understood way to turn raw sequence into something a classical model can learn from. The pipeline filters by antibiotic class (tetracycline being the first target), using PATRIC's 2021 metadata and a GPT-assisted antibiotic classification scheme to organize labels, then splits data 64/16/20 into train/validation/test. 以 Kedro 編排的 PySpark 流程，把每個基因組拆解成重疊的 k-mer（短 DNA 子序列，k=11），並統計其出現頻率作為特徵——這是把原始序列轉化成傳統模型可學習輸入的標準做法。流程按抗生素類別篩選（四環素為首個目標），使用 PATRIC 2021 年後設資料，並以 GPT 輔助建立的抗生素分類方案整理標籤，再以 64/16/20 比例切分訓練／驗證／測試集。

Approach 2 · genomic BERT classifier方法二 · 基因組 BERT 分類器

A second component runs a pre-trained BERT-family model directly over raw nucleotide sequences from .fna (FASTA) files — no hand-engineered k-mer counting, just the sequence itself, optionally split by contig. The model classifies each sequence and exposes its hidden-state features per token, which are saved alongside predictions for downstream analysis. This is the more experimental of the two arms: it asks whether a language model's learned representation of DNA already captures what k-mer counting has to be told explicitly. 第二個部分讓一個預訓練的 BERT 系列模型直接讀取 .fna（FASTA）檔案內的原始核苷酸序列——不經人手設計的 k-mer 計數，只有序列本身，可選擇按 contig 分開處理。模型為每條序列分類，並輸出每個 token 的隱藏狀態特徵，連同預測結果一併保存供後續分析。這是兩條路線中較實驗性的一支：它探問的是，語言模型對 DNA 學到的表示，是否已經隱含了 k-mer 計數需要明確告知模型的資訊。

§ 02 / Status現況

Still open仍在進行

This is an active research project, not a finished product — both arms are still being extended, and the two approaches haven't yet been rigorously compared against each other on the same held-out set. That comparison, once it exists, is the natural next thing to add here. 這是一個持續進行中的研究專題，並非已完成的產品——兩條路線都仍在延伸中，而兩種方法目前尚未在同一個保留測試集上做過嚴謹的正面比較。一旦這個比較完成，將會是本頁自然而然的下一步補充內容。
